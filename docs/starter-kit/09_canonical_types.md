This document defines the canonical list of article types used in the encyclopedia.
It exists to keep naming consistent even before every type has a fully defined infobox schema.

## 1. Canonical Type List

1. **administrative_division**: political or territorial subdivision
2. **artifact**: sapient-species-made historical object
3. **belief_regime**: organized system of beliefs
4. **book**: published written work
5. **character**: fictional person in narrative context
6. **person**: real historical person (in the context of this wiki)
7. **currency**: medium of economic exchange, or coinage
8. **deity**: divine or mythological being
9. **dynasty**: hereditary ruling family or house
10. **historical_period**: broadly defined time span
11. **event**: specific historical occurrence
12. **historical_region**: culturally defined geographic area
13. **institution**: formal social organization
14. **law**: codified legal rule or constitution
15. **military_unit**: organized armed formation
16. **ordinance**: official regulation or decree or proclamation or charter
17. **polity**: organized political entity
18. **settlement**: inhabited community, from commune to capital
19. **species**: biological classification unit
20. **structure**: built physical construction
21. **technical_concept**: specialized abstract idea
22. **treaty**: formal international agreement

## 2. Composition Guidance

- Types may share infobox field groups through composition.
- Shared field groups should be explicit and reusable.
- Type-specific fields must remain declared in their respective schema.

## 3. Scope

This list is the canonical reference for `type` values in frontmatter.
It does not require full infobox coverage for every type at this stage.
